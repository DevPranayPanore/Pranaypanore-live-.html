Introduction to LED (Light Emitting Diode) - The Engineering ...LED (Light Emitting Diode) is a semiconductor device that emits light when an electric current flows through it. It's a highly energy-efficient and versatile technology used in various applications, from lighting to displays and sensors.

There are various types of LEDs, including:
High-Power LEDs: Designed for applications requiring high brightness, such as floodlights and spotlights.
RGB LEDs: Allow for a wide range of colors by combining red, green, and blue LEDs.
Chip-on-Board (COB) LEDs: High-power LEDs that are mounted directly onto a circuit board.


A white light LED (Light Emitting Diode) is a type of LED that emits light perceived by the human eye as white. Unlike traditional incandescent bulbs that naturally emit a broad spectrum of light, LEDs emit light in very narrow wavelengths. Therefore, producing white light from an LED typically involves a combination of methods to create the full visible spectrum.

The blue LED excites the phosphor, which emits yellow light. The combination of blue (from the LED) and yellow (from the phosphor) light is perceived as white. RGB White LED Uses red, green, and blue LEDs mixed together to produce white light. Can be tuned to change color temperature or generate any color of light. May have lower luminous efficacy and stability compared to pc-LEDs.
